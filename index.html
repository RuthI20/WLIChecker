What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure that uses your height and weight to determine if you're a healthy weight.

UnderweightHealthy WeightOverweightObeseSeverely Obese
Below 18.518.5 to 24.925 to 29.930 to 39.9BMI 40 +

You can calculate your BMI using the NHS BMI calculator:
https://www.nhs.uk/health-assessment-tools/calculate-your-body-mass-index/

A high BMI can increase the risk of serious health conditions like type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and certain cancers.
More on obesity: https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/obesity/

Healthy Eating When Trying to Lose Weight

A healthy, balanced diet is essential to maintaining a healthy weight. This includes fruit & vegetables, wholegrains, staying hydrated and avoiding processed and unhealthy foods.

  • NHS Eat Well
  • Healthy Eating for Weight Loss
  • Healthy Stockport

Exercising When Trying to Lose Weight

The NHS recommends that adults are active for 150 minutes each week. Being active benefits mental and physical health. Small changes like taking stairs or walking instead of driving can help.

  • NHS Get Active
  • Healthy Stockport Move More
  • NHS Live Well Exercise

If you are inactive and have health conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, joint pain, COPD, depression or have had a heart attack/stroke, you may be eligible for support at the gym:
Physical Activity Referral in Stockport (PARiS)

Weight Loss Management

If you're obese and struggling to lose weight despite best efforts, weight loss medications may be considered. These include both NHS and private options: NHS Obesity Treatment

Am I eligible for weight loss medications?

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) is the most effective NHS medication, but has strict eligibility. It is prioritised for patients who are severely obese with multiple health conditions. This may change as access increases.
